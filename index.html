Impactful Portayal of the Human Cost of Dead Internet Theory

Published January 23rd, 2026 by "MLYP" | 19 min watch | 8/10

I have never really been afraid of death. I don't have any delusions about trying to stop it or control it. I do fear that my legacy would be infamous or that my legacy would be someone elses generative play-thing. After watching this video, I felt fear and sadness about where we are with technology. For those of you that have read Dungeon Crawler Carl, imagine being in one of those buildings at the start of the Collection only to be twisted into a charicature for the amusement of others.

I see this as impersonation, frankly. In the Halo game series, an alien parasite known as the Flood wreak havok upon the universe. The parasite would take over your body but absorb your consciousness into an eternal hive mind. You knew what was happening but you couldn't stop it. Even if your zombified form expired, you didn't actually die. You would forever live in the hive mind like cloud storage of your consciousness.

The game Black Ops 2 contains a scene where the main character looks at real time ad placement that works the faces of the characters into the advertisement of the product. You watch yourself talk about the benefits of XYZ brand and how much happier your AI personification is with the product. There is a quote attributed to Theodore Roosevelt that "comparison is the thief of joy" and this comparison is already playing out in our world. As a kid when I watched that scene I only perceived it as a jab at aggresive advertising and opulence. I didn't see the horror until data privacy became a personal concern. Now, I can't help but feel paranoia about practices to consume and steer your life with presentations of your digital AI doppleganger.

Like I said, I never really feared death. I have had nightmares since I was young about being stripped of my autonomy as something else assumed total control while I watched in horror. The video goes over the AI abuse and ingestation of Charlie Kirk and I can't help but see the similarity between the two. I can't speak for the dead and no good person would make the dead speak but I would rather die than be a souless slave, powerless to speak for myself.

“If I'm going to die, I want to still be me” ― Peeta Mellark from The Hunger Games by Suzanne Collins
